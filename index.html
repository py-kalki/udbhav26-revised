UDBHAV'26 is a high-intensity, 24-hour hackathon hosted at Sage University, Indore, bringing together top student innovators to build real-world solutions under extreme pressure.

QUICK ANSWERS

Got Questions?

Everything you need to know before you build.

UDBHAV'26 is a high-intensity, 24-hour national-level hackathon hosted at Sage University, Indore — organized by Alta School of Technology. It's designed to push the boundaries of student innovation through rapid prototyping, creative thinking, and intense problem-solving under real-world pressure.

Open to all university students across India. Whether you're a developer, designer, or aspiring founder — if you're a student and hungry to build, you belong here. Cross-college teams are encouraged.

Teams must have a minimum of 2 and a maximum of 4 members. You can assemble teams across different colleges, branches, or disciplines — diversity in skills is a competitive edge.

A nominal per-team registration fee applies to ensure committed participation and cover operational costs across the 24-hour hackathon. Check the registration portal for the exact fee breakdown.

High-speed Wi-Fi, dedicated workspace, overnight power backup, expert mentorship, and a full fueling cycle — dinner, midnight snacks, breakfast, and lunch — so you can focus on what matters: building.

10 problem statements drop simultaneously at the start. Each team picks one and locks in their challenge — no going back. This is where the real game begins.
