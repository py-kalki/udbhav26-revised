Arrival & Check-In

Timeline
8:00 AM – 9:30 AM on 25 April 2026
10:00 AM sharp — be seated in the Seminar Hall by 9:30 AM
Process
ID Verification → Attendance → Kit Handover → Room Allocation

After collecting your kit and room allocation, proceed directly to the Seminar Hall for the pre-orientation assembly. All team members must complete this process within the check-in window.
Important Do not arrive late. Teams that miss the check-in window without prior communication to the organisers risk losing their slot. If you anticipate a delay, contact us immediately.
02 —

Identity Verification

Every team member must carry BOTH of the following documents at the time of check-in. Entry will not be permitted without valid identity proof.

Government Photo ID
Aadhaar Card, Passport, Voter ID, or Driving Licence
College / Institution ID
Valid student ID card issued by your college or university
Zero Tolerance Misrepresentation of identity or institutional affiliation at verification will result in immediate disqualification of the entire team, with no refund. Refer to the Code of Conduct for full details.

24-Hour Timeline
The complete 24-hour event timeline — from doors open at 8:00 AM on 25 April to the closing ceremony at 11:00 AM on 26 April — is published on the official website. Check the website before the event for any last-minute updates or announcements.

04 —

PS Drop

10
Problem Statements
5
Max Teams per PS
15 min
Selection Window

Step-by-Step Process

1
Attend Orientation First
Participate in the full orientation session in the Seminar Hall. The PS Drop happens after orientation — approximately at 10:45 AM.
2
Be at Your Hacking Station by 10:45 AM
All team members must be at their designated stations when the PS Drop goes live. The selection window is strictly 15 minutes.
3
PS Drop Happens on the Website
Visit udbhav26.in/ps at drop time. All 10 problem statements are revealed simultaneously. You will need your Team Code to access and select a PS.
4
First Come, First Served — Any Team Can Pick Any PS
Each PS can be claimed by a maximum of 5 teams. There is no allocation based on Round 1 results — all 50 shortlisted teams compete equally.
5
Selection is Final
Once your team confirms a PS, it cannot be changed. Your entire 24-hour build must align with the chosen problem statement.
Timing Note The 10:45 AM PS Drop time is tentative and subject to change. The organising team may adjust the exact timing depending on how orientation progresses. Any change will be announced live at the venue. Stay in the hall until officially directed.
Technical Issue? If the website is slow, unresponsive, or you encounter any error during PS selection — do not panic. Connect with a nearby volunteer immediately. Every issue will be resolved promptly, and no team will be unfairly disadvantaged due to a technical fault on our end.
05 —

IoT Track — Early PS Drop

Participants on the Smart Cities & IoT track require physical sensors, microcontrollers, and hardware components to build their solution. Sourcing hardware takes time. To ensure IoT teams arrive fully prepared, their problem statement will be revealed two days before the main event.

All Other Tracks
25 Apr
PS Drop at ~10:45 AM on event day
Smart Cities & IoT Track
23 Apr
Early PS Drop — 2 days before the event
IoT teams will receive their PS via official channels (email + WhatsApp) on 23rd April 2026. Use the two days to procure sensors, modules, and any hardware your solution requires. Arrive at the venue on 25th April ready to build.
Important Although the PS is revealed early, the build window starts at 11:00 AM on 25th April — the same as all other teams. The early drop is strictly for hardware procurement. No development or prototyping should begin before the official hackathon start time.
06 —

Judging Criteria

A detailed judging rubric — covering all evaluation criteria, weights, and scoring guidelines — will be provided to all shortlisted teams separately.

Watch for official communication via email and WhatsApp for the Judging Criteria document.
07 —

Mentor Guidelines

For teams that opted for the Mentor Session (additional ₹300 fee), detailed guidelines on how to approach, interact with, and get the most out of your mentor sessions will be communicated separately.

All participants are reminded that interactions with mentors and judges must remain professional and respectful at all times. Soliciting preferential treatment or advance disclosure of judging criteria is strictly prohibited per the Code of Conduct (Section 5.3).
